A Tokyo opening, then six days north
30 October – 6 November 2026

In welcome of Aaron & Michelle Brown
Hosted by Tomoko & Gerhard

A journey arranged around the table — Michelin counters, wood-fire kitchens and snow-country ryokan, strung along the roads and rice plains of Niigata as the maples turn.

It opens in Tokyo over a three-star dinner, then runs north through Gunma and into Echigo: gorges and shrines, a craft town, a crossing to Sado, and a final lunch by the station before the journey home. A private car and driver carry the party throughout.

一
Day One · Sunday 1 Nov

Into the snow country

東京 → 魚沼
Tokyo → Uonuma · approx 3 h 30
Morning · Transfer Reserving
North by private car
Tokyo → Gunma → Echigo

Your car and driver for the week collect you in Tokyo and turn north, leaving the city behind for the rice plains and mountains of Gunma and Niigata.

12:00 · Lunch Confirmed
Sushi Obana鮨おばな
Tatebayashi, Gunma · Sunday lunch only

A quietly revered Edomae counter in rural Tatebayashi — a Tabelog Silver sushiya that opens its doors only for Sunday lunch. Hand-formed nigiri in the Tokyo tradition, well off the tourist map. That Sunday-only window is exactly why it anchors the first day.

En route · Activity Visit
Shōrinzan Daruma-ji少林山達磨寺
Takasaki, Gunma

The hillside Zen temple that gave Japan the Takasaki daruma — the round, red, wish-granting doll made in the town below. A calm first stop in Gunma, the German architect Bruno Taut among its past residents.

En route · Activity Visit
Tomioka Silk Mill富岡製糸場
Tomioka, Gunma · UNESCO World Heritage

Japan's first modern silk-reeling mill, built in 1872 with French engineering and now a UNESCO World Heritage site. The long brick filature hall traces the country's leap into the industrial age, a fitting prelude to the craft days ahead.

Evening · Stay & Dinner Confirmed
Satoyama Jūjō里山十帖
Ōsawa Onsen, Minamiuonuma · 2 rooms · dinner 19:30

★ Dinner is at the ryokan's Michelin-starred SANABURI (早苗饗) — vegetable- and wild-mountain-vegetable-forward cooking by a Kyoto Kichisen-trained chef, following the old seventy-two micro-seasons. The inn itself is a black-beamed farmhouse turned design landmark. Crossing the pass tonight balances the drives: today longer, tomorrow shorter.

二
Day Two · Monday 2 Nov

Gorge, cellar & duck

魚沼 → 新潟の海辺
Uonuma → coast · approx 2 h, broken by the gorge
Morning · Activity Confirmed
Kiyotsu Gorge — Tunnel of Light清津峡
Tokamachi · timed entry · ~30 min from the ryokan

One of Japan's three great gorges, its tunnel ending in the mirror-pool artwork that frames the cliffs and the autumn maples. A timed entry is reserved in advance; early November is peak foliage here.

Morning · Activity Visit
Hakkaisan Yukimuro八海山雪室
Uonuma-no-Sato, Minamiuonuma

The "snow room" at the Hakkaisan brewery's countryside complex, where a thousand tonnes of winter snow hold the cellar at a steady chill to age sake through the year. A shop, café and tasting sit alongside — a short hop from last night's ryokan.

Lunch Walk-in
Miyanoya Soba八海山 宮野屋
Hakkaisan · Minamiuonuma

Soba under the Hakkaisan name, on the brewery's home ground in the snow country. Hand-cut buckwheat and local mountain fare, with the brewery's water and rice all but underfoot — an easy, regional lunch before the drive to the coast.

Afternoon · Activity Visit
Yahiko Shrine彌彦神社
Yahiko, Niigata

Niigata's most revered shrine at the foot of sacred Mt Yahiko, its wooded approach lit with maple colour at this time of year.

Dinner Confirmed
Kappou Chōkichi割烹 長吉
Nishikan, Niigata · BYOB welcome

A country kappo famous well beyond Niigata for its wild duck — birds taken by net in the surrounding rice fields, the breast seared on a hot plate and finished in a deep duck-and-negi soup. Unpretentious, generous, and a short run from the night's ryokan; you're welcome to bring your own wine.

Stay Confirmed
Shaga-no-Sato Yumeya著莪の里 ゆめや
Iwamuro Onsen · 2 rooms

A small garden-onsen ryokan in the old spa village of Iwamuro — quiet rooms, private bathing and an unhurried night.

An innovative French kitchen turning Niigata's produce into a contemporary, course-led lunch — a refined midday table set between the morning's craft visits and the afternoon's sake. Booked for noon. View on the map →

Afternoon · Activity Visit
Imayotsukasa Sake Brewery今代司酒造
Niigata City · founded 1767

A storied Niigata brewery, founded in 1767 and known today for all-koji brewing and one of the region's most welcoming cellar tours. A walk through the brewhouse and a tasting flight round out the day of craft before Tsukioka.

Evening · Stay & Dinner Confirmed
Bettei Koshi-no-Sato別邸 越の里
Tsukioka Onsen, Shibata · 2 suites

An all-suite ryokan in Tsukioka's amber-tinted hot spring. Its kaiseki, taken in-house, leans on Shibata's farmland and the Sea of Japan. Suites, onsen and dinner together, with nothing to organise.

Day Four · Wednesday 4 Nov

Shrine, soba & the city

Tsukioka → Niigata City · approx 1 h 20
Morning · Activity Visit
Asaiino Shrine旦飯野神社
Agano, Niigata

A serene, much-loved local shrine — a quiet start to the day, pairing naturally with the soba lunch a quarter-hour on.

Lunch Optional
Teuchi Soba Kishichi手打ちそば 鬼七
Gosen · no reservations · queue at 11:30

A Michelin-listed soba house in Gosen with a mossy garden view, cutting its buckwheat by hand each morning. There are no reservations — the line forms at the 11:30 opening, where the driver can put your name down. Closed Mondays and the fourth Tuesday; today works.

Afternoon · Stay Confirmed
Hotel Nikko Niigataホテル日航新潟
Bandaijima · 2-night base (Nov 4 & 5)

A high tower on Bandaijima with wide views over the harbour and river mouth — your base for two nights, a short walk from the Sado jetfoil terminal.

Dinner Confirmed
Kyodai Sushi兄弟寿し
Furumachi, Niigata City · closed Sun

★★ Two Michelin stars in the old geisha quarter of Furumachi — Niigata City's finest counter. Edomae craft applied to Sea-of-Japan fish, with the region's own rice and vinegar. A Wednesday seating works around its Sunday closure.

五
Day Five · Thursday 5 Nov

A crossing to Sado

新潟 ⇄ 佐渡島
Jetfoil · approx 67 min each way
Morning · Crossing Optional
Sado jetfoil佐渡汽船
Niigata (Bandaijima) ⇄ Ryotsu

The fast jetfoil skims across to Sado in a little over an hour. A car or guide is arranged on the island for the day. The sea can cancel sailings in rough weather, so a mainland fallback is held in reserve.

Lunch Optional
Benkei Honten廻転寿司 弁慶 / Chozaburo
Sado · Benkei open Thu

Sado's celebrated conveyor-sushi — the island's morning catch over 100% Sado Koshihikari rice. Famously good and famously good value, exactly the island lunch the day calls for. Benkei is open Thursday; Chozaburo (長三郎鮨) is the quieter alternative.

Dinner Confirmed
UGA宇呀
Furumachi, Niigata · single 18:00 seating

An eight-seat wood-fire counter that opened late in 2025 — "port-town Niigata gastronomy" by chef Naoki Susa. Everything is cooked over open flame in one nightly seating at six, water, earth and fire as its theme. Hard to book, and worth booking first.

Stay Confirmed
Hotel Nikko Niigataホテル日航新潟
Bandaijima · night 2 of 2

Back to the harbour tower for the second night.

Weather fallback Optional
Northern Culture Museum北方文化博物館
Konan · open Thu

If the crossing is cancelled, the grand former residence of the Itō family — a sprawling merchant-landlord estate and garden — makes a fine mainland alternative.

Quick Reference
  • 💴Cash is usefulCarry some yen — a few counters and the ryokan extras prefer cash. ATMs at 7-Eleven and Japan Post accept foreign cards.
  • 🍁Early November · cool autumnCrisp days, roughly 10–18 °C, with peak foliage in the hills. Pack light layers and a warm jacket for the evenings.
  • 📱Suica (mobile wallet)Wallet app → tap + → search Suica. Covers metro, bus and JR across the trip.
  • ⏰Time zone: JST = UTC+9No daylight saving · 19 hours ahead of Honolulu.
  • 🔌Electricity: 100V · Type ANorth American travellers need only a 3-to-2-prong adapter, no converter.
  • 📡eSIM recommendedActivate before landing: Airalo, HolaFly or Ubigi.
Dining Etiquette
  • 🚫No fragrance — criticalAvoid perfume, cologne and scented products on dining days. Even a trace disrupts the chef and other guests — the single most important point at high-end Japanese restaurants.
  • ⏰PunctualityArrive on time or early — the meal begins together for everyone. Allow generous transfer time to quiet back-street addresses.
  • 💍Protect the counterRemove rings, bracelets and watches at sushi counters — many are single pieces of precious hinoki cypress.
  • 💰No tippingNot customary in Japan. Say Gochisousama deshita after the meal — deeply appreciated.
  • 🔒Reservations are firmOnce confirmed, reservations are commitments — cancellations damage relationships built over many seasons.
