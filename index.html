Passage North

Tokyo · Gunma · Niigata · Sado
Six days · 1–6 November 2026 · by private car & rail

Begin

One continuous arc north — from a Tokyo morning up through the maples of Gunma, over the mountains to the Sea of Japan, and out across the water to Sado. The trip is written around the table: counters and kaiseki, wood-fire and hand-cut soba, two Michelin-starred sushiya and a crossing for the island's morning catch. Four travellers — Aaron and Michelle Brown, hosted by Tomoko and [your name] — a car and driver throughout, and four nights under ryokan and harbour-view skies.

The itinerary follows below, day by day.
一
Day One · Sunday 1 November

Tokyo to the Tanigawa mountains

Passage Tokyo → Gunma · by car, about 2h45
Morning
Limousine departs Tokyo

North out of the city on a quiet Sunday road, into the rice country of eastern Gunma.

Lunch
Sushi Obana 鮨おばな
Tatebayashi, Gunma

A Tabelog Silver counter that opens its doors for lunch on Sundays alone — which is precisely why it anchors the first day. Confirmed.

Afternoon
Ashikaga Flower Park あしかがフラワーパーク
Ashikaga, Tochigi

A short walk through the gardens before turning northwest toward the mountains.

Evening Overnight
Bettei Senjuan 別邸 仙寿庵
Tanigawa Onsen, Minakami

A Relais & Châteaux ryokan beneath Mt. Tanigawa — eighteen rooms, each with its own open-air spring bath, and a kaiseki dinner that stands in for any restaurant. Two rooms for the two couples.

二
Day Two · Monday 2 November

Through the gorge to the wine coast

Passage Gunma → Niigata coast · by car, about 3h, broken by the gorge
Morning
Kiyotsu Gorge · Tunnel of Light 清津峡
Tokamachi, Niigata

An early start drops you onto the Kan-etsu and out at the gorge — its mirrored tunnel framing maples at their early-November peak. Reserve a timed entry.

Lunch
Cave d'Occi カーブドッチ
Kakudahama · Niigata Wine Coast

A vineyard estate above the Sea of Japan. Reserve a specific table — the French room, the wood-fired grill, or the Japanese counter.

Afternoon
Yahiko Shrine 彌彦神社
Yahiko, Niigata

Niigata's most revered shrine, its approach lit with autumn colour, a short way from tonight's lodging.

Dinner
Kappo Watanabe 割烹 渡辺
Maki, Nishikan · Niigata

Refined Niigata kappo cuisine, twelve minutes from the ryokan in the same ward.

Night Overnight
Shaga-no-Sato Yumeya 著莪の里 ゆめや
Iwamuro Onsen, Niigata

A quiet, garden-set onsen ryokan on the western coast.

三
Day Three · Tuesday 3 November Culture Day

The craftsmen of Sanjo

Passage Iwamuro → Sanjo → Tsukioka · by car, about 1h35
Morning
Marunao マルナオ
Sanjo, Niigata

Ebony and rosewood worked into octagonal chopsticks by hand — an open workshop in the heart of Japan's metal-and-wood craft district, open through the holiday.

Lunch
UOZEN 魚善 ★★
Sanjo, Niigata

Two Michelin stars, set among the rice fields — a tasting drawn almost entirely from the land within sight of the table. Reconfirm holiday hours.

Evening Overnight
Bettei Koshi-no-Sato 別邸 越の里
Tsukioka Onsen, Shibata

An all-suite ryokan in Niigata's amber-watered hot spring, with its kaiseki dinner taken in-house.

四
Day Four · Wednesday 4 November

Shrine, soba, and into the city

Passage Tsukioka → Agano → Niigata City · by car, about 1h20
Morning
Asaiino Shrine 旦飯野神社
Agano, Niigata

A serene hillside shrine known locally as a place of quiet power, after a slow onsen morning.

Lunch
Teuchi Soba Kishichi 手打ちそば 鬼七
Gosen, Niigata

A Michelin-listed soba house with a mossy garden, fifteen minutes from the shrine. No reservations — put your name down right at the 11:30 opening.

Afternoon Two-night base
Hotel Nikko Niigata ホテル日航新潟
Bandaijima · Niigata City

A high tower on the harbour, ten minutes from Furumachi and a step from the Sado terminal — your base for the next two nights.

Dinner
Kyodai Sushi 兄弟寿し ★★
Furumachi · Niigata City

Two stars in the old geisha quarter — Niigata's finest counter.

五
Day Five · Thursday 5 November

A crossing to Sado

Passage Niigata ⇄ Sado · by jetfoil, about 67 min each way
Morning
Jetfoil to Sado 佐渡へ
Niigata Port · Ryotsu

An early sailing from the terminal beside the hotel, out across the strait to the island.

Lunch
Benkei Honten 廻転寿司 弁慶
Sado · or Chozaburo 長三郎鮨

The morning's catch over Sado rice — the island's celebrated sushi, then a single nearby highlight before the return.

Dinner
UGA 宇呀
Furumachi · Niigata City

Wood-fire gastronomy at an eight-seat counter, a single seating cooked over open flame. Books out far ahead — reserve first.

If the sea is rough
Northern Culture Museum 北方文化博物館
Konan · the weather alternative

Should the crossing be cancelled, a grand landowner's estate and garden stands ready in its place — open Thursdays.

六
Day Six · Friday 6 November

A last garden, and the road home

Passage Niigata → Tokyo → Haneda · by Grand Class rail & car
Morning
Old Saito Villa 旧齋藤家別邸
Nishiōhata · Niigata City

A merchant family's villa and stroll garden, coffee, and a short walk through the lanes of Furumachi.

Lunch
Chiyozushi 千代鮨
In front of Niigata Station

A final sushi lunch steps from the platform — no detour before the train.

Afternoon Departure
Grand Class to Tokyo, then Haneda
22:00 · ANA to Honolulu

The Joetsu Shinkansen's first-class car to Tokyo, a private car on to Haneda, and the night flight onward.

Before the journey

What to secure, and what to confirm

Reserve first — hardest seats

  1. Bettei Senjuan — two rooms
  2. Bettei Koshi-no-Sato — two suites
  3. UGA — eight seats, one seating
  4. Kyodai Sushi — ★★
  5. UOZEN — ★★
  6. Kappo Watanabe
  7. Sushi Obana · Hotel Nikko, two nights
  8. Chiyozushi · 鬼七 is a queue, not a booking

Worth a confirming call

  • Kiyotsu Gorge — book a timed entry; early November is peak.
  • UOZEN — reconfirm its hours on Culture Day.
  • Cave d'Occi — choose and reserve the specific restaurant.
  • Sado — Benkei is open Thursday; confirm Chozaburo if preferred, and hold the weather alternative.
